Mercury

Mercury is the first planet from the Sun and the smallest planet in our solar system and nearest to the Sun, Mercury is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is Earth's closest planetary neighbor. It's one of the four inner, terrestrial planets, and it's often called Earth's twin because it's similar in size and density.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun. Mars is also a dynamic planet with seasons, polar ice caps, canyons, extinct volcanoes, and evidence that it was even more active in the past.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun, Jupiter is, by far, the largest planet in the solar system - more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest planet in our solar system. Like fellow gas giant Jupiter, Saturn is a massive ball made mostly of hydrogen and helium.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, and has the third-largest diameter in our solar system. It was the first planet found with the aid of a telescope.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest-known Solar planet from the Sun. In the Solar System, it is the fourth-largest planet by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet.
